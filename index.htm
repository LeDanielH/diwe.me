It is never a question of whether something is, or is not possible. To me everything is possible, it is only a question of time.
I take help from others as a bonus!
I don’t avoid problems for the sake of the peace. I solve them, instantly if possible. I have learned by experience, that consequences of lying or not solving them are usually much worse. I have great relationships with those who understand this concept.
I overslept couple of times when working on the fishing boat. After too few hours of sleep, I had no breakfast and went straight from bed to the freezer hall to sweat in -20°C. I ate 4 hours later. That’s what I call a wake up call! The lesson? Having 30 minutes of extra sleep isn’t worth it.
First comes an idea and then I learn whatever is necessary  in order to bring that idea to life. I’ve done that with my own projects and I intend to do the same thing with yours.
 Since I was 12, I have participated in several sports (athletics, swimming, rowing) I had many injuries because I worked too hard. When I managed not to injure myself, I became a junior Czech Record holder and I have won 2 Czech national championships. My fishing boat experience could be described  this way: moving 60 tons a day, 7 days a week, for three months straight, usually sick and in pain.
I’m purpose driven. I want to do something useful, something that matters, I want to be proud of my work. Money is a secondary motivation for me.
You can read about what I had to overcome in my LIFE OF A WARRIOR story that can be found on my website  BERINGSEAVERSUS.ME.
I was never afraid to make a decision or take responsibility. It is one of my strongest virtues. I learned by experience that I need to make a decision and make the most out of it... and never look back. I don’t follow the “what IF” God.
I’ve learned a great lesson, that leading by example is the best way to get people moving.

Daniel Hoksza Born in USA, California 15.2.1988 Two citizenships: US and Czech I’m a son of  Czech immigrants, born in USA,  my family moved to Czech Republic when I was two years of age. I’ve stayed there until I graduated high school. I then moved to Ireland where I worked as a chef and prepared for entrance examinations for Faculty of Physical Education and Sport at Charles University in Prague. I was 22nd of all people that applied. While studying  there I was also working full-time in Jurys Inn Prague as a porter. My salary wasn’t big enough to pay for my living expenses and I had little time to study.
For this reason, I have decided to quit the school after 6 months and go for an adventure.  I used the last money I had to pay for my flight to Seattle and after 6 weeks I got a job on a fishing boat  in the Bering Sea. I made a lot of money and I decided to use them to develop myself through my own projects. I’m now on a verge to become a Full Stack Web Developer a profession I’m very passionate about.
According to some, I should make myself look as modest and humble to make myself look good. But I would be lying because I’m not. I had a very rough childhood, my father went to prison and my family was close to living on the street. I had a very little chance of having a decent life. Until I managed to make myself some money in Ireland and used them to fly to Dutch Harbor to get one of the hardest jobs in the world.  Which I did. I broke a record by having the fastest pay raise ever among greenhorns. In high school, I was a Czech National record holder and double champion as athlete too. No matter what I did, I always aimed for the highest challenge.
  Sometimes I managed to overcome it, sometimes I failed. E.g. like I did with my website BERINGSEAVERSUS.ME, which was meant to be my business and make me independent. I had many injuries too, some with longterm consequences - preventing me to work on a fishing boat again. I took risks. I’m a father now and I can’t make decisions just for myself anymore. I can’t afford to fail at being a father. It means I need to be the best at what I do and be a great team player in order to give my son a better life than I had. Given time and opportunity, I will do just that. I think that makes me a very perspective proposition to any potential employer.
